I have been playing football for roughly eightteen years. I started when I was six and have played ever since. It has become something that is ingrained in me and I can no longer live without some form of a connection to the game. Watching, Playing, Coaching, etc. Even the weightlifting aspect of the sport has become something that over the years, through continued improvement I have grown to love and partake in everyday.

With football being such a large part of my life it has taught me so many things about myself over the years. Discipline to the game and other parts of my life; the meaning and value of hardwork and what it takes to achieve the goals you set;and what true love is... Though it is never easy, and things get hard, but when everything lines up there is no better feeling in the world for me.

As a man I have grown to appreciate the simpler things of life, and have grown to appreciate other people as they are and respect that everyone comes from different backgrounds. I am a winner of the Hartford Healthcare Courage Award, and am extremely grateful for the opportunity to accept the award.

The Hartford Healthcare Courage Award is presented to individuals, primarily student athletes who have overcome great feats or obstacles in their life as well as exceeding expectations in their particular sport as well as in the classroom.
